Adoption Checklist for new Adopters


Congratulations for getting a pet! You are starting a fantastic and fulfilling relationship. We've created a checklist to help ensure a seamless transition because adopting a new pet involves many changes for both the pet and the pet parent.


Inquiries for Every Adopter:


  • What kind of reactions might your existing pets have to a new addition?
  • Is the pet you're thinking about a good fit for your present home?
  • How will your duties at work or in your social life impact your capacity to take care of a pet?
  • Do you have a strategy in place for your new pet while you travel for business or vacation?
  • What is the opinion of your roommates regarding the presence of a pet in the home?
    • Would you rather have a less needy, more independent pet or one that follows you around the house?


    Time Considerations:


  • Pets require two to three meals a day, or more frequently if they are puppies, as well as access to fresh water at all times.
  • A responsible pet owner should provide their pet with direct attention for at least one hour each day. This could involve playing, exercising, grooming, training, or, in the case of cats, just lounging on the couch for lap time. Dogs will require multiple daily trips outside to relieve themselves.
  • An overly active pet requires more time for exercise and engaging toys to keep them occupied.
  • To avoid matting, pets with long coats require 20 minutes of daily brushing.
    •

    Size Considerations (for Dogs):


  • What size dog can you fit in your house?
  • If your dog ends up becoming larger than you anticipated, will you have enough space?
  • Which size pet would be appropriate for the other residents or guests who frequently visit your home?
  • When deciding on the size of your next pet, is there anything else you need to think about?
  • What size pet can you bring on a trip without discomfort?
  • Pet Costs
  • The adopting organization will almost certainly charge a fee to cover part of the expenses associated with taking in unwanted or lost animals. The amount of money you will spend on your pet during their lifetime will be far smaller than the adoption fee you pay.
  • Before bringing your adopted pet home, you might have to pay to have him or her spayed or neutered.
  • All pets must occur certain costs, such as:
    •
  • Food
  • Routine veterinary care
  • Licensing according to local regulations
  • Collars, leashes and identification tags
  • Kitty litter and box
  • Basic grooming equipment and supplies.
